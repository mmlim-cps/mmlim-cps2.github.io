~ Spring Rolls ~

History

The history of Vietnamese spring rolls is not something agreed on by all. The specific ones showcased here are Vietnamese spring rolls, characterized by their rice paper wrap. While the initial idea of spring rolls originated in China, the Vietnamese version was created in Vietnam. Some believe that the origin of these spring rolls is China, in the sense that Chinese immigrants had brought over their version of spring rolls which inspired the Vietnamese spring rolls, while others believe it is Vietnam, for they say that the two spring rolls are entirely different and that the rice paper used in Vietnamese spring rolls were made by Vietnamese people.
In China, spring rolls were created as a way to celebrate the first day of spring, giving it the name Spring rolls. Around 300AD, spring rolls were like pancakes. As the culinary world advanced, chefs began rolling the pancakes into what we know as spring rolls today.


Recipe

Ingredients Directions
  • Sauce
    • Hoisin Sauce
    • Sweet and Sour Sauce
    • Vinegar
    • Peanut Butter
  • Rolls
    • Rice Noodle(thin)
    • Rice Paper
    • Green Leaf Lettuce
    • Carrot
    • Beansprout
  • Optional
    • Tofu
    • Shrimp
    • Mint
    • Basil
    • Peanuts
    • Hot Sauce(typically Siracha)
 Sauce
  1. Boil hoisin sauce, sweet and sour sauce,
    peanut butter, and vinegar.
    • Prepare beforehand to ensure
      it is cooled down
Roll
  1. Boil thin rice noodles until cooked and let it rest in a strainer for 5 minutes
  2. Spread it out on a plate to let it dry
  3. Shred carrots thinly
  4. Wet rice paper with hot tap water on both sides
  5. Place rice paper on cutting board or similar flat surface
  6. OPTIONAL line cooked shrimp on wrapper
  7. Crack the white stem of lettuce and place it face down on the wrapper.
    Ensure ends of lettuce stick off the sides of the wrapper
  8. Lay over
    • small handful of carrots
    • small handful of bean sprouts(on top of carrots)
    • small handful of dried rice noodles
    • OPTIONAL 2 long pieces of pan fried tofu(protein substitute for shrimp)
    • OPTIONAL 2 pieces of mint of basil, one on each side of the springroll
    • small piece of lettuce on top
  9. Make sure hands are wet to prevent sticking
  10. Make a small fold on one end of wrap
  11. Tightly wrap the rest of the roll
  12. OPTIONAL If the rice paper rips, use another wet wrapper.
  13. Cut diagonally in half and serve with peanut sauce
  14. OPTIONAL Top peanut sauce with crushed peanuts and serve with hot sauce
